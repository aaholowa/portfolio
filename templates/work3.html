003 : PCB Projects

Description: As an active member of my robotics club, I have been heavily involved in designing significant PCB projects for our robots. One of the projects I worked on was the battery leveling board, which aims to monitor all four battery levels and distribute power according to the battery with the most life. This ensures that the robot can operate efficiently without any interruption. Furthermore, I have been working on the control board, which is currently under development and expected to be ready before the Robosub 2023 competition in San Diego, California. I am excited to be chosen to be one of few electrical members to represent our club at Robosub 2023. Designing a PCB involves specifying the required electrical components, documenting the design process, using software such as Altium to design the PCB and test the design with LTspice. Once the schematic and testing phases are completed, the PCB board is sent for manufacturing, and parts are ordered. We primarily use the Arduino Teensy for most of our boards, and we test firmware in C++. After manufacturing and testing, the PCB board is integrated into the robot's system and undergoes further pool testing. If everything works as intended, the board is then implemented into production and utilized in the competition. Overall, designing and implementing PCB projects are integral components of building robots, requiring careful design, testing, and implementation to ensure the robot operates efficiently and effectively.
